The Challenge

McHenry County College's new Center for Agrarian Learning was supposed to be promoted to local highschools through in-person meetings and discussion forums. With Illinois campuses at all grade levels close, and group gatherings currently prohibited, how could the Center get its message to the public?

The Solution

Video presentations were chosen to replace the in-person events. I worked with Sheri Doyel to shoot video of her segments. Because we were only a two person group, it was acceptable for us to meet on campus and shoot at selected locations. These locations were chosen to highlight the content of each of the three video segments. We filmed in the greenhouse for the segment on growing local vegetables with hydroponics, at the outdoor growing houses, called tunnels, for the year-round growing segment, and out in one of MCC's growing fields for the segment on sustainable, grass-fed cattle.

We had one other problem, though. Each segment also featured a local expert on their farm demonstrating the techniques that Sheri discussed in her introduction. Normally, I would go to those locations and shoot those segments, as well. However, due the social distancing rules in effect I was not able to access those locations as I normally would. Therefore, we knew we had to rely on the featured guests to shoot their own segments, and we knew they would be using cellphones.

Our solution? Shoot a tutorial video with some tips for getting good smartphone video. And yes, I shot it on a smartphone. The original video is about 5 minutes, but here I broke it up into its three main segments.
