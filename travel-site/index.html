Summer Palace

Once the imperial garden of the Qing dyansty, The Summer Palace is located in Northwest Beijing. While the name implies a singular structure, the Summer Palace is actually a collection of buildings, gardens, and lakes.

Pavillions, towers, and bridges dot the landscape. The original structures were built beginning in 1153. Construction continued through the centuries. Periodically, from 1271 all the way to 1895, additional buildings, waterworks projects, and refurbishing was conducted.

While the Summer Palace is a popular tourist destination, it is also popular with locals given its rich history.
