Great Wall

Perhaps the most well-known of all China's landmarks, the Great Wall is the setting for an unfortunate movie of the same name. They must have backed up the proverbial dumptruck full of money in Matt Damon's front yard. An uninspired script and bad cgi creatures did a terrible disservice to this truly spectacular landmark.

Hey, but seriously folks, this is one sight to behold.

Begun as a series of walls meant to keep out nomadic raiders, the Great Wall was formed over many centuries. The Great Wall is approximately 5500 miles long, with almost 4000 miles being actual wall and the remainder of that span a series of trenches and natural barriers such as rivers and rivers.

Pro tip: bring some comfortable shoes if you're planning on taking the full tour.
