Independent Short Film Makers - SF

During my final two years in San Francisco, I joined ISFM to share my experience with aspiring film makers in the Bay Area. It was a loosely knit group, and all were welcome, regardless of their experience level. I primarily provided technical guidance and post production assistance. Below are some of the shorts in which I took a more active role.

Pushing My Buttons

This is the short in which I was most engaged. I helped the director with shot selection and storyboarding. I also served as a camera operator on some shots, edited the short, and created the music.

Waxed

The is episode one of what was envisioned as a web series about a soldier returning home to find that his former girlfriend is engaged to his best friend. This was by far the most ambitious project undertaken during my time at ISFM. While I wasn't active in the pre-production or production phase, I was brought in to edit the footage.

There were quite a few challenges due to the shooting style. The entire project was shot in a hand held style with the camera moving among the actors. This didn't provide much coverage, and often times I had to cut around the movement to get reaction shots that properly conveyed the feeling of the scene.

Fem Wars - monologue

This is one of the first things I shot for ISFM. We did a camera training session at a studio, and as part of the training session we had an actor perform a monologue. This also provided an opportunity for everyone to get experience directing an actor. This is the take that I shot during the exercise.
